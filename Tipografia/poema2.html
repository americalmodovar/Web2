Red

A handful of wolvesall cream pelts
and sloping shoulders appear with girls

in red, jaws snapping like capes;

with silver spoons the girls eat the air.


Grow teeth the size of axes there is something
you likein this dream grasses parting like the
sea before Moses.


A sense of law in the way wolves run
through the hissing waver

the girls are not the wolves’ dream—

they vanish into feather beds
when the pillow is turned over.


—Anne M. Doe Overstreet
