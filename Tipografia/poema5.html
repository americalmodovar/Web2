“She had the

day to wonder”

She had the day to wander so she strolled the forest edge, heard
the mirth of fairies long at play, saw a rope-swing gaily swaying

on a limb, inviting her to step into the woods

and ask, Can I come? Can I play?


They taught her fairy tales and sang hand-clapping games down
by the brook—but morning-glory petals started folding—day was
closing, evening coming and with heavy steps she had to turn away.
I cannot play. I cannot play.


—Monica Sharman
